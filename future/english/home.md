# Future of AI

## English Abstract

This wiki is a guided research notebook on the future of AI. It tracks major forecasts, disagreements, risks, upside claims, and governance ideas across a small but high-signal corpus.

## Current Synthesis

The current corpus is already polarized around a central tension: some authors argue that advanced AI could unlock an extraordinary positive-sum century, while others argue that building superintelligence on current paradigms is an existential mistake. The wiki is structured to preserve that tension rather than resolve it prematurely.

The strongest early pattern is that almost every optimistic path still depends on alignment, governance, and institutional competence. Even the upside-focused material treats uncontrolled deployment as a decisive bottleneck.

## Supporting Evidence

- [[english/sources/2024-dario-amodei-machines-of-loving-grace#summary]] argues for a "compressed 21st century" if powerful AI is aligned and deployed well.
- [[english/sources/2025-dario-amodei-adolescence-of-technology#summary]] reframes the near future as a civilizational stress test with concrete risk categories and defenses.
- [[english/sources/2025-yudkowsky-soares-if-anyone-builds-it-everyone-dies#summary]] argues that current methods lead by default to human extinction if they reach superintelligence.
- [[english/sources/2014-nick-bostrom-superintelligence#summary]] supplies the foundational control-problem vocabulary still visible across newer arguments.

## Disagreements And Uncertainties

- It is unresolved whether current model progress extrapolates to highly capable autonomous systems soon enough to dominate near-term planning.
- The corpus disagrees sharply on whether alignment is difficult-but-feasible or effectively unsolved under current methods.
- The corpus also disagrees on whether society should steer, regulate, pause, or stop frontier development.
