# AI Visionary Eliezer Yudkowsky Interview

## Summary

This file is a Russian-language translation of a John Horgan interview with Eliezer Yudkowsky. It is less important as a direct technical source and more important as a worldview source. It shows how Yudkowsky presents himself outside a formal alignment paper: as a theorist of intelligence, decision-making, rationality, and institutional frustration.

For the wiki this source does three things. First, it gives a Russian-language entry point into the broader corpus. Second, it provides background texture for Yudkowsky's later harder claims. Third, it contains policy and epistemic remarks that help explain why later doom arguments are not just emotional alarmism but part of a larger style of reasoning.

## Structure Map

- Opening profile: who Yudkowsky is and how the interviewer frames him.
- Rationality and Bayes: why correct reasoning matters and why evidence strength has mathematical structure.
- Governance and policy: how Yudkowsky thinks about laws, incentives, and reform.
- AI worldview: singularity, Friendly AI, and institutional skepticism in a lighter register than later texts.

## Translation and Framing

Because this source is a translation, it has two layers of mediation: Horgan's framing and the Russian translator's framing. That means it should be treated more carefully than the primary English essays and books. Still, it is highly useful for bilingual navigation and for understanding tone and self-conception.

## Governance and Institutional Change

The interview includes striking remarks about economic policy, bureaucracy, and the difficulty of making systems work well. These are not yet the same thing as AI-governance proposals, but they help explain why Yudkowsky later sounds so pessimistic about institutional competence.

## Rationality and Bayesian Reasoning

A major portion of the interview is about reasoning method rather than AGI timelines. That matters because Yudkowsky sees rationality not as a personality trait but as a discipline of getting reality right. This epistemic style later underwrites his willingness to make sharp AI-risk claims.

## AI and Singularity

The interview contains the recognizable early-Yudkowsky bundle: singularity concerns, Friendly AI, skepticism toward mainstream institutions, and a belief that unusual or uncomfortable ideas can still be true. It is less detailed than the later book, but it supplies historical continuity.

## Timeline and Forecast Claims

- No sharp calendar forecast is given here.
- The source is best read as a worldview and epistemic background document rather than a timeline document.
