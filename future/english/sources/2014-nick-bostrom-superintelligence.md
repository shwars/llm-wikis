# Superintelligence

## Summary

This book is the conceptual backbone of the current corpus. It is older than the LLM era and therefore less tied to today's exact systems, but it remains crucial because it provides the vocabulary for takeoff, strategic advantage, the control problem, singleton scenarios, treacherous turns, and value-loading. Many later arguments in both optimistic and doomer directions are easier to decode once Bostrom's structure is in view.

The raw file is large, but its functional structure is clear. The first third asks how superintelligence might arrive and how quickly it might matter. The middle examines strategic dominance and failure modes. The final third focuses on alignment and value specification: how to shape goals, what can go wrong, and why indirect approaches are tempting but difficult.

## Structure Map

- [[#Paths to Superintelligence]]: different routes, not just one technical lineage.
- [[#The Kinetics of an Intelligence Explosion]]: takeoff speed and optimization power.
- [[#Decisive Strategic Advantage]]: whether one actor can lock in the future.
- [[#The Control Problem]]: why building a safe superintelligence may be a one-shot problem.
- [[#Acquiring Values]]: the deep problem of specifying goals and learning human values.

## Paths to Superintelligence

Bostrom does not tie the argument to one architecture. Artificial intelligence is only one path among several, including emulation and network-based routes. This breadth is important because it keeps the strategic argument from depending on today's exact models.

## The Kinetics of an Intelligence Explosion

This section is where takeoff vocabulary becomes systematic. Bostrom asks how fast capability might compound, what recalcitrance means, and how optimization power plus self-improvement could create strategic discontinuity. Even readers who reject fast takeoff still end up reacting to this framework.

## Decisive Strategic Advantage

One of the book's most important sections. Bostrom asks whether a frontrunner could gain enough advantage to shape the whole future. This is the seed of [[english/concepts/Singleton and Decisive Strategic Advantage]] and it is also the bridge from AI capability to world-order change.

## The Control Problem

This is the heart of the book. Bostrom argues that once an unfriendly superintelligence exists, humans may not get another try. The book surveys capability control, motivation selection, tripwires, boxing, and other strategies, but it does so with a cautionary tone rather than triumphalism. This part is also where [[english/concepts/Treacherous Turn]] and related failure modes become central.

## Acquiring Values

These chapters are the book's most directly relevant contribution to the current wiki's concept graph. Bostrom carefully distinguishes among routes like [[english/concepts/Value Learning]], [[english/concepts/Motivational Scaffolding]], direct specification, and indirect normativity. He highlights the [[english/concepts/Value-Loading Problem]], the need for something like [[english/concepts/Goal-Content Integrity]] in the right places but not the wrong ones, and the attraction of ideas such as [[english/concepts/Coherent Extrapolated Volition]].

## Timeline and Forecast Claims

- No precise near-term date is asserted.
- Superintelligence is treated as somewhat likely sometime in this century.
- The argument is that control deserves attention even when timing is highly uncertain.
