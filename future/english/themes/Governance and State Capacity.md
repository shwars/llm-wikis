# Governance and State Capacity

## English Abstract

This page tracks the political side of the future-of-AI debate: what laws, institutions, and collective actions could meaningfully shape the transition.

## Current Synthesis

Governance appears in the corpus in two competing modes. Amodei emphasizes transparency legislation, evaluation norms, and democratic capacity-building. Yudkowsky and Soares emphasize pause or shutdown logic because they doubt normal governance can move quickly enough. Even the lighter interview material reinforces a deeper skepticism about whether institutions do what reformers intend.

- It is unresolved whether transparency-first regulation is enough for systems approaching superintelligence.
- The corpus contains more normative proposals than enforcement models.
