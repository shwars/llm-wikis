# Optimism vs Doom

## English Abstract

This page tracks the central worldview split in the current corpus: whether advanced AI is best understood as a conditional route to radical flourishing or as a default route to catastrophe.

## Current Synthesis

Amodei and Yudkowsky agree that powerful AI would be historically discontinuous and morally enormous. Their disagreement is over whether humanity can steer the transition in time. Amodei's optimism is conditional on alignment, transparency, and institutional action; Yudkowsky's doom frame treats those conditions as too unlikely under present methods and incentives.

## Disagreements And Uncertainties

- The conflict is not simply risk appetite; it reflects different beliefs about alignment tractability and institutional competence.
- Bostrom's framework supports either camp depending on how pessimistically the control problem is read.
