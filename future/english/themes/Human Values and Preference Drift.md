# Human Values and Preference Drift

## English Abstract

This page tracks one of the hardest questions in the corpus: what human values are, how they change, and why that makes them difficult to specify for advanced AI systems.

## Current Synthesis

Bostrom's value-loading chapters supply the conceptual map, while Yudkowsky's podcast and interview material stress that human preferences are unstable, reflective, and not cleanly captured by surface behavior. The combined picture is that values are not a small annotation problem but a deep target-definition problem.

## Disagreements And Uncertainties

- The corpus contains multiple reasons values are hard, but few concrete modern solutions beyond broad indirect approaches.
- It remains unclear how much preference drift in humans should be mirrored, resisted, or idealized in machines.
