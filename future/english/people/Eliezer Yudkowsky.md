# Eliezer Yudkowsky

## English Abstract

Eliezer Yudkowsky is the clearest extinction-risk maximalist in the current corpus. He argues that superintelligence built with anything like current methods will kill everyone by default.

## Current Synthesis

Yudkowsky combines technical pessimism, institutional pessimism, and moral urgency. He is willing to say openly that alignment may be beyond what current actors can solve in time, and that pause or shutdown efforts are therefore more realistic than optimistic steering narratives.

## Supporting Evidence

- [[english/sources/2025-yudkowsky-soares-if-anyone-builds-it-everyone-dies#summary]] states the default-extinction thesis.
- [[english/sources/2023-eliezer-yudkowsky-dwarkesh-podcast#society's response to ai]] captures his moratorium politics.
- [[english/sources/2023-eliezer-yudkowsky-dwarkesh-podcast#what will ais want]] and [[english/sources/2016-john-horgan-eliezer-yudkowsky-interview#rationality and bayesian reasoning]] show the worldview behind the argument.

## Disagreements And Uncertainties

- His confidence in the default doom outcome is stronger than the evidence style used by more empirically oriented sources.
- The corpus does not yet contain his strongest technical writing, only a book, a podcast, and an interview.
