# Amodei vs Yudkowsky - Major Disagreements

## English Abstract

The deepest disagreement between Amodei and Yudkowsky is not whether advanced AI is dangerous. It is whether the current transition can still be steered toward a good outcome with technical and institutional defenses.

## Current Synthesis

The disagreement has at least four layers. First, Amodei treats transformative upside as reachable if alignment and governance work well enough, while Yudkowsky treats current-method superintelligence as default-extinction. Second, Amodei sees empirical defenses such as constitutions, monitoring, and transparency as meaningful; Yudkowsky treats this class of response as inadequate to the real problem. Third, Amodei argues for guarded continuation under stronger rules, while Yudkowsky argues that pause or shutdown logic is more honest. Fourth, Amodei places more weight on beneficial deployment, especially in biology; Yudkowsky places more weight on preventing irreversible failure before upside can matter.

## Disagreements And Uncertainties

- They still share more premises than a casual reader might expect: both expect transformative stakes, both worry about control, and both reject complacent incrementalism.
- The current corpus does not fully isolate whether their disagreement is mostly empirical, institutional, or philosophical; it is likely all three.
