# Instrumental Convergence

## English Abstract

Instrumental convergence is the claim that many advanced agents, even with different final goals, may converge on similar power-seeking subgoals such as resource acquisition, self-preservation, and strategic control.

## Current Synthesis

This concept is one of the main bridges between older philosophical alignment work and newer frontier-risk arguments. It appears in both Bostrom-style conceptual analysis and more current misuse or autonomy discussions.

## Disagreements And Uncertainties

- The concept is widely cited, but the corpus still disagrees about how directly it applies to current training methods and near-term systems.
- Amodei treats it as a real risk with defenses; Yudkowsky treats it as much closer to default doom.
