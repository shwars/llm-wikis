# Hard Calls and Easy Calls

## English Abstract

Yudkowsky and Soares distinguish hard calls, where details are deeply uncertain, from easy calls, where the right abstraction makes the outcome predictable enough for action.

## Current Synthesis

This concept is the epistemic backbone of the book. It explains why the authors deny precise timetable knowledge while still claiming confidence about the fatality of building superintelligence soon.

- [[english/sources/2025-yudkowsky-soares-if-anyone-builds-it-everyone-dies#introduction: hard calls and easy calls]]

## Disagreements And Uncertainties

- Whether AI doom is truly an "easy call" is one of the central disputed premises of the corpus.
- Optimistic sources accept discontinuity but reject the same level of confidence in catastrophe.
