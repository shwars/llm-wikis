# Grown, Not Crafted

## English Abstract

Yudkowsky and Soares use this phrase to argue that modern AI systems are not carefully engineered in the classical sense; they are trained into opaque internal structures that humans do not fully understand.

## Current Synthesis

This concept explains why current-model safety feels qualitatively different from ordinary software assurance. It also makes many optimistic alignment stories look weaker, because we are steering emergent systems rather than writing explicit rule-based machinery.

## Supporting Evidence

- [[english/sources/2025-yudkowsky-soares-if-anyone-builds-it-everyone-dies#part i: nonhuman minds]]

## Disagreements And Uncertainties

- The concept does not imply impossibility by itself.
- But it strongly raises the evidentiary bar for claims that we can specify and verify the internals we need.
