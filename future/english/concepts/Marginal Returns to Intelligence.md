# Marginal Returns to Intelligence

## English Abstract

Amodei proposes that the AI age should be analyzed in terms of the marginal returns to intelligence: how much additional progress becomes possible when intelligence itself is no longer scarce.

## Current Synthesis

This concept is one of the hidden engines of the optimistic case. It reframes forecasts away from generic "automation" and toward a domain-by-domain question: where does more intelligence still unlock progress, and where do other bottlenecks take over?

## Disagreements And Uncertainties

- The concept is strongest where discovery is insight-limited rather than purely physical-world-limited.
- If institutional, experimental, or legal bottlenecks dominate, the return to intelligence falls.
