# Hail Mary Pass

## English Abstract

In this corpus, a Hail Mary pass means a desperate, low-confidence rescue strategy that might still be worth trying because ordinary plans are judged insufficient.

## Current Synthesis

Yudkowsky uses this phrase in the Dwarkesh interview to describe proposals like human intelligence enhancement, large interpretability prizes, and other unconventional interventions. The key point is not optimism. It is the opposite: the situation looks bad enough that only weak, nonstandard rescue branches remain visibly available.

## Disagreements And Uncertainties

- A Hail Mary pass is not a plan with known reliability.
- Different readers may disagree on whether the phrase clarifies the strategic situation or overstates desperation.
